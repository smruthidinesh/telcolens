Aurora Telecom — Q3 2025 Earnings Summary

Aurora Telecom reported total revenue of $4.21 billion in Q3 2025, up 8.3% year over year
from $3.89 billion in Q3 2024. Service revenue was $3.74 billion, driven by growth in
postpaid wireless and fixed wireless access (FWA) subscribers.

Postpaid phone net additions were 312,000, the strongest quarter in two years. Total postpaid
churn improved to 0.92%, down from 1.07% in Q3 2024, reflecting better retention from the
loyalty program and device upgrade incentives. Prepaid churn was 2.8%, roughly flat year over year.

Average revenue per user (ARPU) for postpaid was $58.40, up 2.1% year over year. Fixed wireless
access reached 1.9 million subscribers, adding 268,000 in the quarter.

Operating income was $812 million with an operating margin of 19.3%. Capital expenditures were
$640 million, primarily for 5G standalone network densification. Free cash flow was $1.02 billion.

Management raised full-year guidance, citing lower churn and accelerating FWA adoption as the
primary drivers. Net debt to EBITDA was 2.6x, down from 2.9x a year ago.
